Explaining what a furry ACTUALLY is

Written by ECSP | 20/7/2025

What is a furry?

  • A furry is someone who represents themselves online or in some real life places like conventions with an animal version of themselves, which is usually humanoid, but how humanoid it is depends on the person that designed the furry persona (AKA fursona).
    • ...and even then, people who do this can also not be furries! Because "furry" is just a tag that unifies an abstract group of people who choose to use it on themselves based on the stuff they like.
  • No, furries don't want to have sex with animals, that's an uncorrelated thing called being a criminal (zoophile).
  • No, furries do not identify as animals or act like animals, that's an uncorrelated thing called therians.
  • No, furries don't wear fursuits literally everywhere (Have you ever been inside a full body suit of any kind? it gets so hot in there).

What do furries like or do then? Personally, I became a furry because I find the humanoid creatures furry artists draw and the techniques they use really cool and super interesting and they inspire me to become a better artist! The stuff furry artists draw is super unique and not really able to happen outside the furry context in my opinion. I really like having this cool dragon creature that I made with myself and my interests as reference represent me online, it makes me feel happy. I also think being able to act a bit like a dragon online is fun and cute and I like "being just a little lizard girl" :) I'd love to have a fursuit so I could also do it at home, but I'm broke, so... (No, I still don't identify as a lizard girl, this is all just for fun!).
But being a furry is all about sharing your interest for these cool humanoid creatures! This is why the furry community highlights very well its talented creative people and you see so much furry media around the internet. Being a furry is just about having fun with and appreciating these cool characters people make :)
